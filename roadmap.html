The Vinyl Scout

Roadmap

Roadmap

Vinyl Scout is built in deliberate phases. Phase 1 is live; the rest are planned and intentionally not started yet — each one is added only once the phase before it is solid.

1

Cataloguing by photo

Live

Build the collection

Browse the whole collection, and add records by photographing them and reviewing the draft before it’s saved. Hand-edit details, replace covers, and remove records one at a time. Editing is private; viewing is open to anyone with the link; the collection is backed up automatically.

2

Pressing accuracy

Planned

Know exactly which copy you own

Look up the precise pressing, label, and catalogue details for each record, so an entry reflects the exact edition on the shelf — not just the album.

3

Wishlist

Planned

Mark albums you want to buy

Keep a separate list of records you’re hunting for — separate from your collection. Mark any record as wanted, sort by price range or condition, and use it as a shopping guide.

4

Pricing & collection value

Planned

Understand what it’s worth

Show an estimated total value of the collection based on market prices, so you know at a glance what your records are worth on the secondary market.

Phases are sequential. A later phase is never started early, and nothing is added to an earlier phase just because it would be easy to slot in.
